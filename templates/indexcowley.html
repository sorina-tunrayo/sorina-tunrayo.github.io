Welcome to our not so complete guide of local hangouts for the self-proclaimed cool kid in Oxford. We have created a list of restaurants, cafes, bars and miscellaneous others for when you need to escape from reality or simply want to dazzle your friends with your knowledge of the chiccest places to be.

The movement

Our aim is to mobilise an army of super cool kids to support local businesses and break the obstinate division of "town and gown".

Tunrayo: As a new memeber of the Oxford graduate community, I, like so amny others have also been overwhelmed by the huge number of activities that the colleges and the Universities provide. Futhermore, my department is exactly 5 minutes from my bed and my college, well...It is so easy to be trapped in the very circle that inevitably forms and thus never see anything beyond its radius.

Trina: I love food so much I spent a term as the Cherwell's Food and Drink editor, but I am far too lazy to cook. So after almost four years here I've found one or two great places I'd like to share with everyone.

Fran: I'm super awesome and I love food and I know things.

We say, who said you can't have "town and gown"?

Places

God, you're lucky. You know why? Because you're oh so close to Cowley, where all the coolest kids eat. That could he you. Here's where:

Restaurants

Taberu:
Fabulous Japanese food, totally worth blowing your student loan on.

Cafe Baba:
Probably better suited to a liquid lunch, but the tapas is great and so is the atmosphere.

Atomic Burger:
Carb-tacular, cheap, and splendidly retro. Enjoy, we do!

Queen's Lane Coffee House:
Cute, quaint and a bit posh, this historic coffee place (the oldest in Europe, so they claim) is the perfect place to take mum for lunch.
